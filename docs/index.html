Fusion 360 Adventures: Professional 3D Modeling

I enjoyed using Fusion, especially compared to Freecad. The software was relatively fun to use, and made me feel productive.

Tutorial Shape

Basic cube with rounded corners and center hole, first dive into Fusion modeling capabilities. Followed tutorial.

Lampshade Sketch

Hand-drawn reference profile in Paint used as the foundation for the 3D lampshade model.

Lampshade - Top View

Top down view showing the curved opening and elegant profile of the custom lampshade design. Created a line tracing, and revolved. Cut the shape with a circle.

Lampshade - Bottom View

Bottom perspective.

Laboratory Flask

Transparent glass flask with white liquid, created an outline using line tools and the circle. Then duplicated the shape, cut the shape, and scaled the shape to meet parameters.

Bowler Hat - Exterior

Classic bowler hat, traced using the line tool then scaled the y axis to meet shape requirements. Cut the edges with a circle.

Bowler Hat - Interior

Interior view demonstrating the hollow construction and proper head fitting dimensions of the hat.

Fusion Fabrication: The Mouse Stand Solution

After identifying the issue in my previous entry I began planning my model. I thought to attach an external area in order to lengthen the space for mouse movement. I cut a whole so that it could directly attach to the computer, reducing external motion. In order to simulate a mouse pad I added a glass texture for smoother movement. I created a square shape cutting a space for the trackpad, and recessing the hole to fit my computer. Additionally, added glass mousepad for improved motion.
